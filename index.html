Problem Space

The resilience of rural communities

In this semester project our task was to design an ecosystem intervention, and formalise that intervention in a digital product. Physical isolation, limited economic diversity, and higher poverty rates, combined with an ageing population, increase the vulnerability of rural communities to any crisis, including the current permacrisis. Systems of fundamental importance to rural populations are already stressed by remoteness and limited access - climate change is only making this worse.

Project Mastering IxD - University Course
Year 2024 fall (MA1.1)

Primary Issues

Effects of climate change: Rural communities tend to rely heavily on climatesensitive resources such as local water supplies and agricultural land; climate-sensitive activities such as arable farming and livestock husbandry; and natural resources such as fuelwood. Climate change can reduce the availability of these local natural resources, limiting the options for rural households that depend on natural resources for consumption or trade. Land may become less fertile; fewer reeds may be available for basketmaking; there may be less local fuelwood for heating. These impacts will progressively increase over this century and will shift the locations where rural economic activities (like agriculture, forestry, and recreation) can thrive. Effects of outbound migration: Rural communities already struggle with outbound migration - leaving them with an ageing population, and lower ratio of economically active population. Climate change, through its negative effects on traditional economic activities, will speed up this process. This in turn, through the reduction of economic diversity, makes these communities more vulnerable - creating a self-exciting point process. Lack of economic resilience: Due to their geographic and economic isolation, rural communities are less resilient from an economic perspective - negative changes in one specific economic area (e.g. agriculture) have a rippling effect on the stability of the entire community. Diversity of economic activities makes communities less vulnerable to crises.

Chosen intervention area

How might we improve access to reliable information in rural communities to combat misinformation and strengthen civic participation?

In many rural areas, limited access to diverse sources of credible information can make communities more vulnerable to misinformation, fake news, and political propaganda. This can distort public understanding, fuel social divisions, and undermine democratic processes. By increasing access to trustworthy news outlets and fostering critical thinking skills, rural residents can become better informed and more engaged citizens. Community-driven initiatives, local media platforms, and information-sharing networks can help promote transparency, encourage dialogue, and support the resilience of rural communities against external manipulation.

A comprehensive mental health and wellness app that helps women embrace their cycles, maximize their strengths, and navigate challenges with confidence. UI for NIÜ

ABOUT THE PROJECT

An Innovative Web Interface Design to Showcase the Hungarian Innovation Ecosystem

This work was done for a Proposal by the Hungarian Innovation Agency (NIÜ). The mission of NIÜ is to strengthen Hungary's innovation capacity by supporting the evolving needs of the innovation ecosystem, fostering connections between its stakeholders, and promoting innovation-driven culture and mindset. Through the development of the innovation ecosystem map, the NIÜ aims to provide all stakeholders with an accessible, user-friendly platform that consolidates the information, funding, and collaboration opportunities. This initiative supports the realization of innovative projects, fosters connections between universities and companies, and promotes knowledge and technology transfer.

Project Call for Proposals: Designing the UI for the "Hungarian Innovation Ecosystem Map" - 2nd prize
Year February 2025
Team Ágnes Réka Varga, László Majsai

Details

Designing the Experience

Since the target audience of the ecosystem map are the following:

  • University and research institute researchers, educators, and students
  • Founders and employees of startups and innovative SMEs
  • Investors
  • Leaders and R&D professionals from large corporations
  • Business service providers, etc.

There are users who are experts in the field of business, innovation and funding, more than half of the users visiting the page are not very familiar with this world. This means that we have to design an interface where every visitor can find what they are looking for. An easy, accessible website that simplifies this amount of information.
